Mapo Tofu Recipe

Description

Mapo Tofu is one of the main dishes from Sichuan, China.

It is a spicy stewed tofu dish with the silky soft tofu swimming in spicy sauce, and it pairs very well with a bowl of warm rice.

Ingredients

For the sauce:

  • 2 tbsp doubanjiang (Chinese chili bean paste)
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp black vinegar
  • 1 tsp sugar or agave nectar
  • 1 tbsp shaoxing wine (optional)

For Mapo Tofu:

  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 tbsp chili oil (you can substitute with cooking oil)
  • 3 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 2 oz ginger, chopped
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tsp toasted and ground Sichuan peppercorn
  • 400g of ground pork, chicken, beef, or any plant based meat
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 block (14 oz) silken tofu, cut into 3/4-inch cubes
  • 1 tbsp corn starch
  • 1 red chili, chopped (optional)

Steps

  1. Combine all ingredients for sauce in a mixing bowl, set aside.
  2. Heat a cast iron wok over medium heat.
  3. Add cooking oil, chili oil, chopped garlic, ginger, chili and green onions. Stir fry all together about 1 minute then stir in Sichuan peppercorn powder.
  4. Add in your choice of ground meat and breakdown into small pieces. Cook 2 to 3 minutes or until fully cooked and ground edges.
  5. Pour sauce mixture and stir fry sauce, protein and aromatic vegetables together about 2 to 3 minutes.
  6. Pour stock and deglaze bottom of wok. Bring it to boil. Add cubed tofu and carefully stir. You want to keep the shape of tofu as square as possible.
  7. Meanwhile, mix cornstarch and 2 tbsp water. Pour starch mixture into boiling mapo tofu base while carefully stirring. This is very important, because cornstarch mixture will thicken mapo tofu immediately only where it added into.
  8. Garnish with some chopped green onions and extra Sichuan peppercorn powder. Serve with warm cooked rice. Enjoy!
